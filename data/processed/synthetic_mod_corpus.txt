The Department of Military Affairs (DMA), headed by the Chief of Defence Staff (CDS), was created in 2019 to ensure enhanced synergy among the three services—Indian Army, Indian Navy, and Indian Air Force. The DMA is responsible for the optimal utilization of resources through jointness in operations, including the establishment of joint-service theatre commands. The CDS acts as the permanent Chairman of the Chiefs of Staff Committee and is the principal military advisor to the Raksha Mantri.

The Agnipath scheme, launched in 2022, serves as the primary route for recruitment of personnel below officer rank (Agniveers) into the armed forces. Agniveers are recruited for a period of four years, after which up to 25 percent of each batch may be considered for enrolment in the regular cadre based on merit and performance. The Seva Nidhi package, which includes the Agniveer's contribution and matching government contribution with accrued interest, is provided to those who complete their four-year tenure. This scheme aims to lower the average age profile of the armed forces and enhance technical proficiency across the ranks.

The Canteen Stores Department (CSD) operates as an attached office under the Ministry of Defence and is responsible for the procurement and distribution of consumer goods to the armed forces and their families at concessional rates. The CSD's operations are governed by the Board of Control for Canteen Services (BOCCS), chaired by the Quartermaster General (QMG). Every serving and retired defence person is entitled to a Smart Card for CSD purchases, which tracks their monthly grocery and liquor quotas. The CSD also provides a dedicated portal for the purchase of Against Fixed Deposit (AFD) items such as motorcars, motorcycles, and large appliances, ensuring a transparent and automated booking process.

The Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) is the premier agency for military research and development in India, with its headquarters in New Delhi. DRDO laboratories, such as the Aeronautical Development Agency (ADA) and the Combat Vehicles Research and Development Establishment (CVRDE), have developed flagship platforms like the Tejas Light Combat Aircraft and the Arjun Main Battle Tank. The Technology Development Fund (TDF) scheme under DRDO supports startups and MSMEs in developing niche technologies to reduce import dependence and achieve 'Aatmanirbharta' in defence manufacturing.

Procurement of weapons and equipment is governed by the Defence Acquisition Procedure (DAP) 2020. The DAP 2020 emphasizes the 'Buy (Indian-IDDM)' category (Indigenously Designed, Developed and Manufactured) as the highest priority. The Defence Acquisition Council (DAC), chaired by the Raksha Mantri, is the apex body for deciding on the Acceptance of Necessity (AoN) for major procurement projects. Offset obligations apply to all contracts above a specified threshold, requiring foreign vendors to invest a minimum of 30 percent of the contract value back into the Indian defence ecosystem.

The Department of Ex-Servicemen Welfare (DESW) is responsible for the welfare and rehabilitation of veterans. This includes the management of the Ex-Servicemen Contributory Health Scheme (ECHS), which provides comprehensive medical coverage through polyclinics and empanelled hospitals. The DESW also oversees the grant of pension, including the implementation of One Rank One Pension (OROP) revisions, and works in coordination with the Kendriya Sainik Board (KSB) for state-level veteran support.

Disciplinary matters in the Indian Army are governed by the Army Act 1950, while the Indian Navy is governed by the Navy Act 1957, and the Indian Air Force by the Air Force Act 1950. The Judge Advocate General (JAG) branch is the legal wing of the armed forces, providing advice on court martial proceedings and administrative inquiries. Personnel found guilty of serious misconduct may be cashiered, dismissed, or sentenced to imprisonment, as determined by a General Court Martial (GCM) or a Summary Court Martial (SCM) as per the gravity of the offence.

Financial powers in the MoD are decentralized through the Delegation of Financial Powers to Services (DFPDS). This allows formation commanders at the Command, Corps, and Division levels to sanction expenditures for operational requirements and administrative maintenance without constant reference to the Ministry. All such sanctions must be concurred in by the Integrated Financial Advisor (IFA) to ensure audit compliance and fiscal discipline.

The Integrated Defence Staff (IDS) was created in 2001 following the recommendations of the Group of Ministers on reforming the national security system. It facilitates coordination among the services, the MoD, and other security agencies. The IDS headquarters manages the National Defence Academy (NDA) and the Defence Services Staff College (DSSC), ensuring standardized training and professional development for officers across the services.
